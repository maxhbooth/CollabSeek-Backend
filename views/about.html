About CollabSeek

What is CollabSeek?

We know networking in academia can be a difficult, time consuming, and yet ultimately necessary procedure. We aim to make this part of the research process a little easier. This website will help users in the UNC system find collaborators for research projects.

The way collaborators are often found through UNC today is via email blasts that often go unanswered. We believe that the specificity of connecting person to person will mitigate that problem, as people will be spared irrelevant email blasts in favor of being emailed by people familiar with their skill set.

This project is targeted towards the local community. Interested participants will be able to sign up for an account on our webpage if they have a “unc.edu” account. They will then complete a signup process and input their professional information, qualifications, skills, specialties, and available resources. Then they can search using a variety of fields for potential collaborators and reach out to them on their own via the contact information listed on profiles.

How Can I Use CollabSeek?

First you will need to sign up and create an account in order to use the site. When you create your account, you will list your research discipline, specialties, skills, and available resources. Then, if you are looking for a person with a particular skill set, you can search our database of other users to find anyone who has that skill. This site will help you find people who you can then contact on your own.

Who Made CollabSeek?

CollabSeek is the result of the UNC-CH class COMP 523: Software Development with David Stotts. This class pairs groups of students with people on campus who have an idea for a piece of software to further their academic development. The client for this project was Wanda Bodnar, PhD of UNC Gillings School of Global Public Health. The students who worked on the project are all class of 2018 computer science majors: Mac Watrous, Max Booth, Marcus Wallace, and Alden Caron-O'Neill.
